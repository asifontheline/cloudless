The alternative to commercial cloud

The cloud, without the cloud

A community mesh network that can spread across continents with no data centres — it runs on commodity hardware and the systems people already own, and it belongs to the people who run it.

$0 metered billing 0 data centres 5% safe default share 📱 phones & laptops 100% yours
Start a mesh Why it exists

a living mesh: laptops, workstations, spare GPUs — anywhere on earth

Why

Commercial clouds concentrate expensive machines in data centres and rent them back to you — metered by the second, priced across hundreds of SKUs, with egress fees that tax you for leaving. CloudLess inverts the model: the machines a trusted group already owns federate into one mesh with replication, failover, and web management built in.

☁️ Commercial cloud

  • Unpredictable metered bills; ~30% spend wasted on idle resources
  • Egress fees — a lock-in tax on your own data
  • Your data lives in someone else's data centre
  • Whole FinOps teams exist just to read the invoice

🕸️ CloudLess mesh

  • Zero marginal cost — the hardware is already yours
  • No egress fees: data moves inside your own mesh
  • Privacy-first: data never leaves group hardware
  • A ledger of who gave and who used — no bills, just balance
Cooperatively owned · privacy-first · cheap hardware first · honest security (defense in depth, never "foolproof") · everything web-managed, setup in seconds.

Cloud AI is the first service on the mesh — a standard chat-completions API with routing and failover, where cloud cost and privacy pain are highest. Storage, compute, and data services ride the same mesh next.

Run a node

You don't need to know anyone. Run a node, tell it how much of your machine you're willing to share, and it joins the community mesh — contributing only what you allow, and earning you service in return. Here's how, in plain steps.

Step 1 — Start your node.

$ cloudless up
  Console:  http://127.0.0.1:8080/     # your private control panel, in a browser

127.0.0.1 just means "this computer" — it's your own admin page, only you can open it. It is not the address others use.

Step 2 — Find your public address. Every machine on the internet has a public IP — the number the outside world sees. To find yours, open a terminal and run:

$ curl ifconfig.me
203.0.113.9        # ← that's your public IP (yours will be different)

No terminal? Just search "what is my IP" in any browser — the first result shows it.

Step 3 — Publish your node (make it reachable). Your node listens on a port (7946). For others to reach it, that port has to be open to the internet. Pick the path that fits you:

  • Easiest — cloud VPS or a machine with a real public IP: nothing to do. It's already reachable at the IP from Step 2.
  • Home internet — port forwarding: open your router's admin page (usually 192.168.0.1 in a browser), find Port Forwarding, and forward port 7946 to your computer. This tells your router "send mesh traffic to this machine." A 3-minute, one-time setup.
  • Can't forward (mobile data / shared building internet): those connections hide you behind the provider. Use the one-click overlay — in-build (#20) — which connects nodes across any network with no router setup at all.

Step 4 — Set how much you share. Your machine, your rules — entirely your discretion. Every node starts at a safe 5% (barely noticeable, never hot, nothing like a resource-gulping virus), and you can tune it up to 70%. Got an idle spare box? Turn it up. Personal phone? Leave it low.

$ cloudless up                       # 5% — safe default, any device
$ cloudless up -share-cpu 70%        # dedicated / idle machine: turn it up (70% max)

The ceiling is 70%, never 100% — your machine always stays responsive and cool. On phones a thermal & battery guard pauses sharing if it warms up or runs low (charging-only by default). Contribution is metered on the ledger and earns you service in return. Tunable share controls are live; the mobile thermal/battery guard is in-build (#54).

That's it. Your node is now part of the mesh, serving up to your limits and earning you access to everyone else's. Point any standard chat app at http://localhost:8080/v1 to use it; if any node drops mid-request, another takes over automatically.

Sign in — no passwords to remember

When you open your console the first time, you register in one tap using a passkey — your device's fingerprint, face, or PIN. There's no password to invent, forget, or have stolen.

  1. Register: open http://127.0.0.1:8080/ → Create account → confirm with your fingerprint / face. Done. A secret key is created and stored safely on your device — it never leaves it.
  2. Sign in: next time, just touch the sensor. No typing.
  3. More devices: add your phone or laptop by scanning a code — each gets its own passkey.
  4. Lose a device? Revoke it from any other device; a one-time recovery code (saved at signup) restores access if you lose them all.

Passkeys are the phishing-resistant standard (WebAuthn) built into every modern phone and browser — the same thing banks use. Passwordless sign-in is in-build (#52).

One portal for everything. Once you're signed in, your console is your control panel — the single place to both use the mesh (call AI, manage keys, see what you saved vs a cloud bill) and contribute to it (set your share limits, watch your contribution and earned service on the ledger, see the live network map). Same portal on your laptop and your phone. Nothing else to install, nowhere else to go.

Community

The code, the board, and the roadmap are all public. CloudLess is community-owned by design — and you don't need to know Go to contribute. Build in any language against the open API; Python is first-class. See how in the contributor guide.

How code lands: every check-in from anywhere is built, vetted, and tested by CI, reviewed, then merged into main by a queue every 2 hours — parallel work worldwide, and main never breaks. Details in Contribute.
