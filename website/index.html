About

Commercial clouds win by concentrating expensive machines in their data centers and renting them back to you, metered by the second. Cloudless inverts that: spare desktops, old workstations, and consumer GPUs owned by a trusted group — a team, a lab, a co-op — federate into shared infrastructure that behaves like a cloud with none of a cloud's bills, lock-in, or data custody.

Privacy-first: data never leaves group hardware · Cooperatively owned: no vendor · Cheap hardware first: heterogeneity is the design, not a compromise · Honest security: defense in depth, never "foolproof" · Web-managed: everything runs from an embedded console, setup in seconds.

Why: the cloud bill problem

Cloud spend is unpredictable by design — per-second metering, egress fees, idle resources, surprise autoscaling, per-token AI pricing across hundreds of SKUs. Whole teams (FinOps) exist just to understand the bill. Cloudless's answer is structural, not a discount: hardware you already own has zero marginal cost — no meters, no egress fees, no surprise invoices. The only "bill" is electricity, and the group sees exactly who contributed and who consumed.
